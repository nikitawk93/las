About the Editor

Nikita Willeford Kastrinos is a graduate student and predoctoral instructor at the University of Washington. She works on nineteenth-century British literature with a focus on Victorian print culture and book history. She is especially focused on the periodical and other popular publications, the serialized novel, authorship, reading practices, reception, and circulation. Her Master’s thesis, provisionally titled, “‘I saw his form pass by—appealing—significant—under a cloud’: Form(s) of Indeterminacy in Lord Jim,” investigates the ways in which Lord Jim disrupts the gradualist characteristics of nineteenth-century science, history, and literature as epitomized in the installment novel. Looking to Lord Jim as it first appeared as Lord Jim: A Sketch in Blackwood’s Magazine, she reads its anachronistic and asynchronous movements in time and interpret its establishment of a new form of causation, laying out a method of narrative analysis which challenges the teleology of the serial novel and offers a new sense of the form’s true heterogeneity.
